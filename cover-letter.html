Cover Letter

Dear Sir or Madam,

I am writing to apply for the Industrus Engineering Graduate Program. As an electrical engineer with experience on major transmission infrastructure projects, I have developed strong technical, communication and project delivery skills. Please find my response to the selection criteria below.

A commitment to ethical conduct and the highest standards of professional accountability

During design work on a major transmission project, I identified a discrepancy between the issued electrical specifications and the applicable Australian Standards. Despite the document being under schedule pressure to finalise, I knew it was my responsibility to raise the issue before the design progressed any further. I documented the non-compliance thoroughly and formally raised it with the design manager. The design was corrected before issue for construction, avoiding what could have been a costly and potentially unsafe outcome. Although it was not an easy decision given the schedule pressures involved, I believe that raising concerns promptly and clearly is a fundamental part of professional accountability as an engineer.

Demonstrated ability to effectively communicate both with other engineers and with stakeholders from different fields

In my current role within the integration team I work across engineering, commercial, environmental and stakeholder engagement teams daily. During the early phase of the EIS a misalignment emerged between the approach from the environmental team and stakeholder engagement team. I organised and facilitated several cross-team meetings to discuss the issue and come to a conclusion for both teams I also prepared a shared summary document outlining the key interdependencies between workstreams, so each team understood how their decisions affected the others. The teams reached a workable resolution within two weeks, avoiding delays to the project programme.

Demonstrated ability to use and manage information

As part of my integration role, I was responsible for consolidating technical input from multiple discipline team. The information arrived in different formats from each team and needed to be checked for consistency and completeness before it could be compiled. To manage this, I developed a register to track the status of inputs from each team and flagging outstanding. Where gaps or conflicts in the data were found, I followed up directly with the relevant tea. The deliverable was completed on time to the required quality standard.

The ability to manage your own performance in a professional environment

Working on a large infrastructure project means that direct supervision is not always available and tasks regularly evolve with limited guidance on how to proceed. I have regularly been assigned tasks where the next steps are not clearly defined and have had to take ownership and determine the appropriate path forward independently. I used a consistent approach of mapping out the logical sequence of steps at the start of any task, identifying dependencies and potential blockers early so I can plan around them. Where I encounter gaps in information, I reach out to the relevant people rather than waiting for issues to escalate. I have consistently met my deadlines and have been trusted by project managers to progress complex receiving positive feedback for identifying and resolving issues before they are escalated.

A demonstrated ability to work as part of a team and to show leadership when required

During my time on the project, I was responsible for compiling a register of hold and witness points and distributing the inspection requirements to multiple stream leads across the project. Each stream lead had different priorities competing for their attention, which made it important to communicate the requirements clearly and in a way that was relevant to each team. I prepared targeted instructions for each stream lead that outlined exactly what was required of them and why it mattered to the broader quality assurance process. I followed up consistently with each team to ensure the requirements were understood and that the register remained accurate and up to date as the project progressed. As a result, the hold and witness point process ran smoothly across all workstreams, with each team clear on their obligations and the overall inspection programme kept on track.

I look forward to hearing from you and have attached my resume for your consideration.

Regards,
Jessica Lee
